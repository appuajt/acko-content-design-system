# ACKO Content Style Guide — before / after

A tightening and consistency pass so the guide obeys its own rules: tighter, more confident, and internally consistent.

**At a glance:** 19 contradictions resolved · all 30 page descriptions tightened to ≤2 sentences · 134 em dashes → 0 · "Use / Avoid" and "the user" standardised everywhere · channel limits standardised to characters.

---

## 1. Tighter and more confident

**The opening line of the whole guide.**

> **Before** — 4 sentences, hedged
> "We're not a traditional insurance company that talks like a policy. We write for the human on the other end. So, no intimidating jargon or frustrating processes. We're here as a helping hand."

> **After** — 2 sentences, confident
> "We write for the human on the other end, not a policy document. No jargon, no runaround, just here to help."

---

## 2. Rules that read like the brand

**The "Insightful" voice pillar.**

> **Before**
> "Being insightful means being the most informed person in the room — and using that knowledge to help the customer choose well, not to push them toward what we want to sell. Tell users something they didn't know they needed to know."

> **After**
> "Be the most informed person in the room, and use that to help the user choose well, not to push what we want to sell. Tell the user something they didn't know they needed to know."

---

## 3. House style, applied to the guide itself

**The "Error states" description — 134 em dashes across the guide are now gone.**

> **Before** — 4 sentences, em dash
> "Errors are already stressful. The copy should reduce that stress — not add to it. State what happened, why, and what to do next. Then stop."

> **After** — 2 sentences, clean punctuation
> "Errors are already stressful, so reduce that rather than add to it. Say what happened, why, and what to do next, then stop."

---

## 4. The guide now obeys its own rules

**One rule said "always write as 'we'." An example three lines away broke it.**

> **Before** — the "active voice" example contradicted the "we" rule
> Use: "ACKO will transfer ₹12,000 to your account"

> **After** — consistent with the rule it sits beside
> Use: "We'll transfer ₹12,000 to your account"

---

## 5. One product, one answer

**Auto insurance was described two different ways on two pages.**

> **Before**
> Voice page: Auto = "Assuring, fair, steady"
> Auto page: Auto = "Vigilant, Practical, Clear"

> **After**
> Both pages: "Assuring, fair, steady"

---

## 6. A term that was banned and used at once

**The Enterprise page named the product "Group Medical Cover" — and its own Avoid list forbade that exact term.**

> **Before**
> Product table: "GMC (Group Medical Cover)"
> Avoid list: don't say "Group Medical Cover"

> **After** — the real distinction, made explicit
> Companies buy the category, **Group Health Insurance**. The cover their employees receive is the **Group Medical Cover (GMC)**. "Group Medical Cover" removed from the Avoid list.

---

*Every description in the guide now passes its own rules: ≤2 sentences, active voice, sentence case, no em dashes, "the user" throughout.*
